          03 Capability Scoring Pass 1

          A single LLaMA 3.3-70B call at temperature 0.0 scores the company across six weighted dimensions. The bar width below reflects each dimension's weight in the final score.

          AI Products & IP
          25%
          Proprietary AI platforms, patents, and products deployed at scale. Most heavily weighted — reflects true technological ownership rather than intent or aspiration.
          Talent & Research
          20%
          AI team headcount, PhD researchers, peer-reviewed publications, and competitive credentials such as Kaggle leaderboard rankings.
          Client Deployments
          20%
          Real enterprise deployments with documented, measurable ROI. Audited evidence is weighted over marketing case studies. The system explicitly distinguishes claimed vs disclosed outcomes.
          AI Infrastructure
          15%
          Cloud provider partnerships, MLOps tooling maturity, GPU/TPU infrastructure, and data platform capabilities underpinning AI delivery.
          Leadership Vision
          10%
          Board-level AI mandates, executive strategy coherence, and organisational commitment signals — preferencing annual report disclosures over press releases.
          AI Revenue
          10%
          Percentage of total revenue from AI services, year-on-year growth trajectory, and disclosed financial metrics. Highest confidence when sourced from audited filings.
          // score_calculation.formula
          overall_score = (
            ai_products_ip × 25 + talent_research × 20 + client_deployments × 20
            + ai_infrastructure × 15 + leadership_vision × 10 + ai_revenue × 10
          ) ÷ 10
          Each dimension is scored 0–10 by the LLM at temperature 0.0. The weighted sum ÷ 10 yields a 0–100 scale score. All scoring is fully deterministic — the same company analysed twice returns identical scores.

          05 How to Read Both Scores Together

          The capability score and hype score must be read together. A high capability score only means something when the hype score confirms the evidence was credible. Four key scenarios:

          HIGH CAP + LOW HYPE → Strong Signal
          The best outcome. High capability score backed by audited evidence. Evidence base is credible and specific. Suitable for advancing to deeper due diligence or investment thesis.
          HIGH CAP + HIGH HYPE → Verify First
          Claims may be inflated by marketing volume. Score should not be taken at face value. Mandatory: obtain the company's annual report and re-run with annual_report_url provided.
          LOW CAP + LOW HYPE → Honest Assessment
          The company has low AI maturity, but the assessment is credible. The gap is real, not an artefact of data quality. Early-stage or non-AI-focused company.
          ANY CAP + NO AUDIT → Incomplete
          Confidence is automatically downgraded when no annual_report_url is provided. Scores reflect public/marketing sources only. Always provide the annual report for definitive scoring.
